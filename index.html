What Is a DIFC Foundation?

A DIFC foundation is a legal structure created within the Dubai International Financial Centre that holds assets on behalf of beneficiaries. It has its own legal identity, separate from the founder.

This structure is often used for:

  • Wealth preservation
  • Succession planning
  • Asset protection
  • Holding shares or investments
  • Family governance

Unlike a company, a foundation has no shareholders. It is managed by a council and follows rules set out in its charter and by-laws.

Since the DIFC Foundations Law was introduced in 2018, adoption has grown steadily. By 2024, the DIFC reported continued growth in family wealth structures as more international families moved assets into Dubai for stability, privacy, and legal clarity.

DIFC Foundation Setup: Step-by-Step

Setting up a DIFC foundation in Dubai is a structured process handled by experienced advisors.

Basic setup steps

  1. Define the purpose and beneficiaries
  2. Draft the charter and by-laws
  3. Appoint council members
  4. Register with DIFC authorities
  5. Transfer assets into the foundation

Requirements

  • Minimum of two council members
  • Registered office within DIFC
  • Charter outlining objectives
  • Guardian (optional but recommended)

Registration timelines typically range from a few weeks to a couple of months depending on complexity.

UAE Tax Treatment of DIFC Foundations

The UAE introduced corporate tax in 2023, creating new considerations for wealth structures.

A DIFC foundation may be treated as a taxable entity. However, family foundations can apply for special status that allows them to be treated similarly to an unincorporated partnership for tax purposes.

This may allow:

  • Tax neutrality for family wealth holding
  • No corporate tax on passive asset ownership in certain conditions

Professional structuring from the start is important.

DIFC Foundation vs DIFC SPV Setup

Both structures serve different purposes but can work together. Here’s a breakdown:

  1. Purpose
    • DIFC Foundation: Designed for wealth holding and succession planning.
    • DIFC SPV: Functions as an asset holding company for specific investments or businesses.
  2. Ownership
    • DIFC Foundation: Has no shareholders; it is a self-owned legal entity.
    • DIFC SPV: Has shareholders who own the company’s assets.
  3. Use
    • DIFC Foundation: Ideal for family wealth planning and long-term legacy management.
    • DIFC SPV: Typically used for holding business shares, investments, or specific assets.
  4. Control
    • DIFC Foundation: Managed by a council, which can include the founder, beneficiaries, or fiduciary service providers.
    • DIFC SPV: Managed by directors appointed according to corporate governance rules.

Many families structure their wealth by placing a DIFC Foundation at the top to manage long-term wealth and succession, with a DIFC SPV beneath it to hold operating companies or investments for more detailed asset control.
